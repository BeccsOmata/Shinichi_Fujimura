Overview



 

     Shinichi Fujimura was an amateur archaeologist who contributed to the Japanese Paleolithic Hoax, where he dispersed early Japanese Paleolithic Era (30,000 10,000 BCE) and Jmon Period (14,000 300 BCE) artifacts throughout numerous significant archaeological sites.

 

     Fujimura was exposed for his acts of fraud back in 2000 by the Mainichi Shimbun, a Japanese daily newspaper, where journalists from the Mainichi Shimbun set up hidden cameras at an archaeological site that Fujimura was working on, in hopes of catching him in the act of planting artifacts they were successful.


     Prior to him being reprimanded, Fujimuras findings and research were deeply impressionable to the foundational scientific understandings of Japanese Paleolithic analyses. Following the official announcement of his falsities in 2003 by the Japanese Archaeological Association (JAA), Fujimura was then expelled from both the JAA and from his job at the Tohoku Institute


     This website will evaluate Fujimura's efforts in derailing Japanese Paleolithic archaeological records with various stone tools and Jmon Era artifacts, explain how he was able to get away with these major claims of false artifact recoveries, and analyze evidence that supports the claims of Fujimura's findings being a hoax.
